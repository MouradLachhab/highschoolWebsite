International Baccalaureate Program

In essence, École d'éducation internationale de Laval's curriculum is outlined to ensure comprehensive training of its students as prospective members of society. Moreover, its primary purpose is to form open-minded individuals worthy of the twenty-first century. This academic establishment has opted to adhere to the IB Middle Years Program (MYP), which is currently utilized by 943 secondary school establishments amid 87 countries. These schools are supervised by the International Baccalaureate, which is commonly referred to as the IB. Furthermore, since 1988, Quebec schools inducted into this program have been grouped within a cooperative: the SÉBIQ, which is short for la Société des écoles du monde du baccalauréat international du Québec et de la francophonie. This peculiar cooperative has known astounding growth over the last years. To this day in Quebec, 127 secondary schools offer the IB Middle Years Program.

The Areas of Interaction

Areas of interaction are vehicles through which students explore different perspectives of an issue. They are embedded in the program to help students develop a proficiency for research. Students get to understand the differences and similarities that exist between the various methods used to acquire knowledge though these different viewpoints. The five areas of interaction are:

  • Approaches to learning
  • Community and service
  • Human ingenuity
  • Environments
  • Health and social education

The IB Learner Profile

The IB learner profile is the IB mission statement. It is a set of ten qualities which are used as ideals that can inspire and motivate students as well as teachers. All elements of the program emphasize the development of these qualities. Students in the program have to aspire to be:

  • Inquirers
  • Knowledgeable
  • Thinkers
  • Communicators
  • Principled
  • Open-minded
  • Caring
  • Risk-takers
  • Balanced
  • Reflective

The IB Mssion

Student growth is at the core of the IB program. Students are invited to develop a plethora of qualities through the program.

  • Develop natural curiosity
  • Develop proficiency in various subject matters
  • Develop an aspiration for a better world
  • Develop a sense of mutual understanding
  • Develop intercultural respect

The Main Concepts

The IB program puts an emphasis on the development of many concepts.

  • Intercultural awareness

    Discover your own culture and that of others.

  • Comprehensive learning

    In-depth study of several disciplines

  • Communication skills

    Facilitate research and understanding

  • Values

    Constructive criticism, Respect, Desire to learn

Community Service

Community service involves many of the main concepts of the international program as well as the areas of interaction and the IB learner profile. Every student is obligated to do a certain number of hours of community service each school year. The school administration imposes a certain number of mandatory hours for each level.

  • Secondary 1: 20 hours
  • Secondary 2: 25 hours
  • Secondary 3: 25 hours
  • Secondary 4: 25 hours
  • Secondary 5: 10 hours

The main goal of community service is to:

  • Demonstrate empathy, compassion and respect towards the needs and feelings of others
  • Work actively to help individuals in need
  • Interact with the outside world
  • Help your community

The Personal Project

Through the final year of the IB program, students have to undertake a project stemming from their initiative and creativity. It is the culmination of a student's progress throughout the last five years spent in the program. Essentially, it is the concretization of the main concepts of the program, of the areas of interaction and of the learner's profile. It is an extensive project ranging from September to February during which the students must invest a great deal of time and effort.

  • 13th competition
  • Takes place from February 20th to 22nd
  • Location: Vanier Cégep
  • 28 attending schools

The Game

  • Stacking objects is the sole objective of the game. A higher heap of objects results in a higher score.
  • The game pieces on the first floor will be foam prisms. Those on the second floor will be buckets containing concrete while those on the third level will be beach balls.
  • There will be 30 prisms, 15 buckets as well as 10 beach balls positioned on each object's corresponding level.
  • Each team may preload a custom-built piece onto their robot. This item's dimensions must comply with the maximum size dimensions of 20cm x 20cm x 20cm.
  • The playing field is a spacious rectangle separated into three floors.
  • Each of the five robots must start at their allotted starting area.
  • All five starting areas occupy one end of the playing field.
  • The third floor will be accessible via a mobile ramp. At the beginning of each play, it will always block one of the two access ramps to the second floor.

Tutorial

Read our tutorial pertaining to a kiosk's construction.

  • Introduction
  • Planning
  • Construction
  • Scheme

A kiosk is a construction which is typically used to display an object or a product. It can take various shapes and sizes as its design necessarily differs depending on the selected theme. This tutorial shall cover the process behind building a basic kiosk such as the one built by our kiosk team.

  • Finding a theme

    Before starting any project, you must begin by selecting a theme. Necessarily, brainstorming is an appropriate option to finding one. In our own case, the team which was working on the kiosk looked for a theme that could be portrayed through a kiosk. Ultimately, it was decided that the main theme would be "The conquest of space" as this subject could easily be illustrated through the robot . Later on, this theme would be used by the video team as their main storyline.

  • Research

    After eventually selecting a theme, you must study the aforementioned in order to find inspiration. For example, the kiosk team watched various videos related to the theme for inspiration. In addition, they went to the Cosmodome to observe items related to space (e.g. dashboards).

  • Planning

    Next off, it is essential to come up with a plan for the kiosk. For instance, the kiosk team selected different wood panels with which they tried various possible arrangements in order to find a practical and aesthetic design. However, they didn’t want to build an ordinary kiosk so they ended up choosing an octagonal scheme. Ultimately, sketches are an important aspect of the planning process. You should always write down every idea pertaining to the setup of the kiosk.

  • The outer sides of the panels

    Plans aside, the foundations of the kiosk must be solid and appealing. For example, our kiosk's panel colors didn’t fit in with the space theme so they decided to paint every panel in black. However, that didn’t please the kiosk team. Therefore, they decided to paint gray squares on the boards by using stencils of various sizes.

  • The inner sides of the panels

    Moreover, the interior of a kiosk is as important as the exterior. For instance, the kiosk team needed a way to decorate the kiosk's interior. Essentially, they brought up the idea of installing lights inside the panels and covering the panels with black fabric that could let the light pass through. However, the fabric was too opaque, and the ambient light was affecting the lights' visibility. Therefore, in order to fix the aforementioned, interference from other light sources was eliminated by making a makeshift roof made of black fabric. Also, in order to plug the lights into an outlet, a hole was drilled at the bottom of each panel. Once all the panels were decorated, the team assembled them to finish the foundations of the kiosk.

  • The dashboard

    At this step, all that is remaining is the interior which needs to be decorated in order to present your product. In our own case, the team worked on a dashboard. The dashboard consisted of three sections of painted cardboard pieces on which were placed various components in order for it to look futuristic. Some components, such as the dials, were printed and glued to cardboard pieces after which they were glued to a cardboard base with a certain angle so that they would pop out off of the dashboard.

  • Features

    It is important to seperate the kiosk into different sections for each feature. For instance, the kiosk team divided the kiosk into different sections for the video, the website, a photomontage, the school presentation as well as a work zone.

    Every section has its own section. In order to view the video, the team decided that they would install a projector above the entrance to project the video above the dashboard. At the right side of the kiosk, a laptop was placed in a decorative pillar with a window for easy access to the computer which would display the website. On both sides of the dashboard, photo montages were presented. As for the working zone and the school presentation zone, they are located behind the entrace panels.
